This work extends the exploratory study on rule-based explainability for thyroid cancer recurrence prediction in three directions. First, the quantum support vector classifier of the reference study is replaced by two families of quantum models that are closer to the current practice of quantum machine learning, namely a Pegasos quantum SVC trained with a fidelity quantum kernel and a variational quantum neural network with ZZFeatureMap encoder and TwoLocal ansatz, so that the comparison with the classical SVC baseline covers both a kernel-based and a variational quantum model. Second, the evaluation moves beyond the ideal statevector setting and adds a noise-aware scenario built on the Aer simulator with the noise model extracted from the real IBM Quantum device ibm\_fez, which gives a realistic prediction of the performance of the quantum models on a NISQ device without spending time on real hardware. Third, the full matrix of five models over three feature subsets is run on the Hercules supercomputer of the CICA through a SLURM-based pipeline with phases and dependencies, and the code of the experiments and of the pipeline is released as a public repository to support reproducibility and to make the comparison with later works easier.
